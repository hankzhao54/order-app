Getting started

Signing in

  1. Open the app link on your phone or computer. On a phone you can “Add to Home Screen” to use it like an app.
  2. Enter your username (for example kh) and your password.
  3. Tap Sign in. You’ll land on the page for your role automatically.
You only type your username — no email needed. If your account uses a real email, type the full email instead. Accounts and passwords are set up by an admin.

What you can see

The menu only shows what your role needs. A store orderer sees ordering; the kitchen sees production; an admin sees everything. If something’s missing, your role may need changing — ask an admin.

Store staff Orderer · Store manager · Bar staff

Your daily routine: 1) Count your stock at the end of the day (Stocktake). 2) Check what's low. 3) Place or top up your order before the Monday cutoff.

Placing a weekly order

  1. Go to Order.
  2. Make sure the type is weekly. The banner tells you which production week this order is for.
  3. Browse by category, or tap ↻ Repeat last order to load your previous order, then adjust.
  4. Tap an item to add it; use − / + to set the quantity. The unit (e.g. pcs, kg) shows next to the name and the quantity.
  5. Review the cart on the right, then tap Submit.
If you submit more than once in the same week, the new items are merged into your existing order for that week (quantities add up) — as long as the kitchen hasn’t started it yet. So you don’t create a mess of duplicate orders.

The weekly cutoff

There’s a weekly cutoff (default Monday 08:00). Orders placed before it count for this week’s production; orders placed after it automatically roll to next week. You can always order — nothing gets locked.

Urgent & event orders

  • urgent — handled right away, not tied to a production week. Use for things you forgot or need fast.
  • event — for catering/events. You can add an event name and date. Handled separately from weekly production.

Templates

Save a recurring order as a template, then load it next time with one tap. Look for the templates row at the top of the order page.

Kitchen Kitchen manager

Your daily routine: 1) Open Kitchen, work the urgent and this-week orders. 2) Use "By item" to see total production needed. 3) Mark items Ready as you finish; send buy-list items to Procurement.

Working through orders

  1. Go to Kitchen. Switch between By order (one store at a time) and By item (totals across all stores — best for production planning).
  2. Orders are grouped into 🔥 Urgent, 🎉 Event, This week’s production, and Next week.
  3. If one store has several orders for the week, they’re folded into one group showing combined totals — tap to expand and work the individual orders.
  4. For each item, mark it Ready, Short (made less than asked), or None (can’t make).
  5. Items marked “buy” go to the purchasing list instead of being produced.

Finishing

  • When all items in an order are handled you can complete it; completed orders stay visible (dimmed) and can be reopened.
  • Producing an item adds it to Central Kitchen stock as a new batch (with today’s date and an expiry based on the item’s shelf life).

Inventory & stocktake Kitchen · Store manager · Bar staff

Doing a stocktake

Why count daily: the system doesn't track each sale. You keep stock accurate by counting what's actually left — the system works out how much was used since last time. Count fast-moving items (sauces, mains) daily and slow ones (dry goods) weekly. Accurate counts = correct low-stock alerts = you reorder the right things.
  1. Go to Inventory → Stocktake. Pick your location if asked.
  2. Tap an item. Enter the actual total you counted using the big number field, then Save count. The system adjusts the batches behind the scenes — you don’t need to think about batches day to day.
  3. Use search and the “only uncounted” filter to track progress (“Counted X/Y”).
  4. Tap ➕ Add items to add something that isn’t on this location’s list yet.

Expiring & expired stock

Items expiring within 7 days show a yellow tag; expired show red. Open the item — expiring/expired batches are listed with a 🗑 Throw button. Throwing reduces stock and is logged as waste.

Sending stock back to Central Kitchen

Stores can return stock to the Central Kitchen: open the item, use ↩ Send back to Central Kitchen, enter the quantity. It leaves your stock immediately; the kitchen confirms receipt under Receiving.

Receiving deliveries

Under Inventory → Receiving you confirm what arrived — both deliveries from the kitchen and returns from stores. Confirming adds the quantity to that location’s stock.

Bar staff only see Inventory for their own bar (e.g. Coca-Cola, drinks). They count and manage their own stock; they don’t order from the kitchen.

Driver / dispatch Driver · Kitchen · Admin

  1. Go to Dispatch. Ready items are grouped per store.
  2. Mark items as dispatched as they go out; this deducts them from Central Kitchen stock (oldest batch first).
  3. Use the slip print for a paper packing list if needed.
  4. “Close week & archive” moves finished orders out of the active view.

Admin Admin

Dashboard

Built for a daily glance, top to bottom: Needs attention first (expired / expiring stock, stores that haven't ordered, low stock, unavailable items) — or "All clear" when there's nothing. Then the key numbers (items to handle, ready to dispatch, buy list), then per-store progress, then quick links. The weekly cutoff setting lives in a collapsed ⚙ Settings section at the bottom.

Catalog (products)

  • Items tab: search, filter by category or status, and sort. Each item has its name, category, Make/Buy, order unit, price, and active toggle.
  • Tap ⚙ Specs on a row to set the stock unit, Per unit content (weight in g/kg or count in pcs/db), shelf life (days), storage, low-stock threshold, and vacuum level (P0–P7 for sealing).
  • Hover the ⓘ on any column header for an explanation.
  • Set supplier prices with the 💰 button — these feed the spend report.

Users

  • Create a user with just a username + password + role (+ location for store-bound roles). The username becomes their display name.
  • Roles: Orderer, Store manager, Bar staff, Kitchen manager, Driver, Admin.
  • Password resets a password; Disable blocks login but keeps records; Delete removes the account permanently.
Use Disable rather than Delete if the person may return or has historical orders. Delete is permanent.

Reports

  • Purchasing spend — what was bought, by item / supplier / store, for a week, month, or all time. Needs supplier prices set in Catalog.
  • Stock log — every stock movement (produced, dispatched, received, returned, stocktake, scrap) with filters, plus total waste/scrap value.

Troubleshooting

The app is stuck loading / behaving oddly

Close it fully and reopen. On a phone PWA, you can also remove it from the home screen and re-add it to clear old cached versions.

I can’t sign in

Check the username and password with your admin. The admin can reset your password from the Users page.

“No ordering role yet”

Your account exists but has no role assigned. An admin needs to set your role (and location) on the Users page.

An item shows no unit / wrong unit

An admin can fix it in Catalog → Items (order unit, and Specs for stock unit / per-unit content).
